About

I am a postdoctoral associate in the Social Cognitive and Neural Sciences Lab (PI: Dr. Jonathan Freeman) at New York University. My work examines the roles ingroup favoritism, stereotyping, and the self play in intergroup face processing. My research takes a multidisciplinary approach, by drawing on theories and methods from social psychology, cognitive and computational neuroscience, and artificial intelligence.

Before coming to NYU, I received my Ph.D. in Social Psychology at UC Santa Barbara, working with Dr. Kyle Ratner in 2021. I completed my undergraduate degrees in psychology and statistics at University of Minnesota, Twin Cities in 2015.

In my free time, I enjoy working out, going to the beach, hiking, playing video games, listening to music and podcast, watching the NBA (go Timberwolves!), vegan cooking, and coding for fun.

Please feel free to contact me via email or Twitter. You can also download my latest CV here.
